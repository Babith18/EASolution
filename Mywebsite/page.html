What is Forex and How Does It Work?

The Foreign Exchange Market (Forex or FX) is the world’s largest and most liquid financial market, where currencies are bought and sold 24 hours a day, five days a week. Forex trading involves exchanging one currency for another with the aim of making a profit based on price movements.

The Forex market operates without a central exchange — it runs through a global network of banks, financial institutions, corporations, and individual traders. Trades happen electronically over-the-counter (OTC), meaning transactions occur directly between parties via computer networks, rather than on a centralized exchange.

Prices in Forex are influenced by factors like:

  • Economic news and reports
  • Interest rates
  • Political stability
  • Global events and trends

Forex offers high liquidity, flexibility, and opportunities to trade with leverage, making it a popular choice for traders worldwide. With the right strategy and tools — like automated Expert Advisors (EAs) — traders can take advantage of market movements day and night.
